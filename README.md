Customer Churn Analysis

Project Overview

This project analyzes customer churn patterns to identify the key factors that influence customer retention and customer attrition. The analysis helps businesses understand why customers leave and provides recommendations to improve retention.

Objectives

Analyze customer demographics, service usage, and billing information.
Identify factors contributing to customer churn.
Visualize customer behavior using charts and graphs.
Provide actionable business recommendations.

Key Findings

Customers with month-to-month contracts showed higher churn rates.
Customers with higher monthly charges were more likely to leave.
New customers were more prone to churn compared to long-term customers.
Contract type and tenure were strong indicators of customer retention.
Recommendations

Encourage customers to switch to long-term contracts.
Improve onboarding for new customers.
Provide targeted retention offers for high-risk customers.
Monitor customers with high monthly charges.
